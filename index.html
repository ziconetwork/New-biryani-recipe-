Royal Chicken Biryani

A Royal Recipe for a Majestic Meal

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:
  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces, bone-in preferred for flavor)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Salt to taste
For the Rice:
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 5 cups water
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1-2 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
  • Salt to taste
For the Biryani:
  • 3 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 2 medium onions (thinly sliced)
  • 1 large tomato (chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds (optional)
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup fried onions (optional, for garnish)
  • Saffron strands (optional, soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken Marinade

Clean and cut the chicken into pieces. Add the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, and spices. Mix well and marinate for at least 1 hour or overnight for richer flavor.

Step 2: Prepare the Rice

Rinse the basmati rice, then boil water with spices. Cook rice until 70% done, then drain and set aside.

Step 3: Fry the Onions

Fry the onions in ghee until golden brown. Drain excess oil and set them aside for garnish.

Step 4: Cook the Chicken

In the same pan, cook the marinated chicken with tomatoes and spices until the chicken is tender and the gravy is rich. Add water if necessary.

Step 5: Layer the Biryani

In a large pot, layer chicken and rice, sprinkle mint, coriander, and fried onions. Drizzle saffron milk (optional) on top.

Step 6: Dum Cooking

Seal the pot and cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes. Let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before serving.

Step 7: Serve

Fluff the rice and serve with raita, salad, or pickles. Garnish with more fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves.

Additional Tips for the Perfect Biryani

Rice Cooking Tip:
  • Cook rice al dente, as it will cook further during layering.
  • Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour or overnight for richer flavor.
  • Fried onions add texture, and store-bought ones are a good alternative.
  • If saffron is unavailable, substitute with turmeric, though saffron gives a unique fragrance.
  • During layering, the chicken should be the dominant layer, with a thin rice layer on top.
